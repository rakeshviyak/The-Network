The Cuff Road Project (TWC2)

Migrant workers who are in dispute with their employers, or are injured, have no family here to fall back on. They are often destitute and unable even to feed themselves. TWC2 operates a food programme in Little India. TWC2′s volunteers are stationed at the food point so that workers have someone to talk to and consult. We give advice on what rights and options they may have, and how they can pursue their cases. Our volunteers also monitor their progress. The statistics that we compile from the cases we see at the Cuff Road Food Programme gives us an overall picture of the kinds of difficulties and the scale of exploitation and abuse workers suffer.

Loction: Isthana Restaurant 1C Rowell Road, Singapore 207958

  • Date: Monday 7.30am – 9.30am
  • Contact: Transient Workers Count Too, PH: 6247 7001

Migrant Workers Picnic

Many migrant workers work in the construction sector and they usually spend their off days gathered at the empty grass patches having picnics. With our social network, Singaporeans can easily join these migrant workers as a group to listen to their concerns about adapting to life in Singapore.

Location: Hong Lim Park, Singapore

  • Date: Friday 7pm – 10pm

Migrant Workers Movie Screening

On Sundays, foreign workers flock to this field in the middle of Sungei Road 'Thieves Market' where outdoor movie screenings are held. Bollywood movies are screened for free from 7PM to 10PM on a white cloth with a projector. The equipment and space is provided by a Singaporean Indian who runs a small hotel in the nearby Tekka area.

Location: Hong Lim Park, Singapore

  • Date: Sunday 7pm – 10pm
  • Contact: Migrant Workers Centre, 6536 2692

Weekly Cricket

Indian workers enjoy a round of their country’s favourite game, cricket, on this empty field next to IMM shopping mall. Most of the workers live in the nearby dormitories at Toh Guan and bring the game to the spaces they inhabit. Using tree branches, these foreign workers set up the wickets for their weekly cricket game on the empty field. The 4-metre long sandy patch is a result of their years of playing cricket here.

Location: Field outside IMM Shopping Mall 2 Jurong East Street 21, Singapore 609601

  • Date: Sunday, 9am – 12pm

Weekly Soccer

Using bottles as goalposts, Malaysian and Burmese workers square off in a game of soccer on a field big enough to accommodate them and the cricket-loving Indian workers who play just 50 metres away.

Location: Field outside IMM Shopping Mall 2 Jurong East Street 21, Singapore 609601

  • Date: Sunday, 9am – 12pm

Aidha Business and Financial Management Course

  5. Advanced Leadership Club: Learn business and management skills

Ministry of Manpower Changi Lodge 2 Outreach Event

The Ministry of Manpower organises events at foreign worker dormitories to inform migrant workers about their employment rights in Singapore. The Ministry also reaches out to employers and other Voluntary Welfare Organizations (VWOs) in Singapore.

  • Contact: Ministry of Manpower Representative, Number: 9789 4041
